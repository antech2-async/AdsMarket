# AdSourcing Pitch Deck Brief

## One-Liner

AdSourcing is an autonomous sponsorship agent pair that turns a sponsor mandate into a negotiated, escrowed, delivered, verified, and settled community sponsorship.

## Short Pitch

Community sponsorship is still manual. A sponsor needs to find a qualified community, check trust, negotiate price, avoid unsafe content, pay before delivery, verify the post, and settle payment. For micro-sponsorships, the manual effort often costs more than the deal itself.

AdSourcing solves this with two accountable agents:

- SponsorAgent represents the brand. It protects budget, content policy, campaign goals, and payment authority.
- CommunityAgent represents the community. It protects inventory, price floor, content rules, and delivery channel.

After setup, the agents handle the commercial workflow: discovery, signed handshake, policy checks, negotiation, escrow funding, delivery, verification, settlement, and proof.

## Core Thesis

> Micro-sponsorship does not need another marketplace. It needs accountable commercial agents.

## Positioning

AdSourcing sits between:

- brand/sponsor mandate;
- community inventory mandate;
- agent identity and reputation;
- signed negotiation messages;
- payment escrow;
- Discord or Telegram delivery;
- proof bundle and payment receipt;
- OpenClaw tool execution.

AdSourcing is not:

- a regular marketplace UI;
- a chatbot that recommends deals;
- a generic payment demo;
- a fully production-ready financial product;
- a replacement for merchant/KYB compliance.

## Narrative Arc

### Act 1 - The Manual Gap

Small community sponsorships are valuable but inefficient because trust, negotiation, policy review, delivery verification, and payment are manual.

### Act 2 - Agent Mandates

Both sides give their agent a bounded mandate. SponsorAgent cannot overspend. CommunityAgent cannot accept unsafe content or low price.

### Act 3 - Autonomous Execution

The agents verify identity, reputation, signatures, and policy before negotiating and funding escrow.

### Act 4 - Payment Before Delivery, Proof Before Settlement

Escrow protects both sides. Delivery proof triggers verification. Settlement creates receipts.

### Act 5 - Demo and Impact

The demo shows the happy path plus a bad-case rejection before escrow. The product is a payment-backed autonomous commerce agent.

## Main Message Hierarchy

1. Sponsorship discovery and settlement are too manual for micro-deals.
2. Agents can execute bounded commercial outcomes.
3. SponsorAgent and CommunityAgent act as separate principals.
4. Deterministic guardrails make autonomy safer.
5. Payment escrow turns negotiation into accountable execution.
6. Proof bundles and payment receipts make the outcome auditable.
7. DOKU Sandbox adds a fiat payment gateway path for the payment track.

- fintech-grade but not corporate stiff;
- agentic but not sci-fi;
- polished enough for a winning hackathon submission;
